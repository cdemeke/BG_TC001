SugarClock

A blood sugar monitor shouldn't cost a fortune.

A simple, open source CGM display for your desk or nightstand.

SugarClock converts a low-cost Ulanzi TC001 smart pixel clock into a dedicated glucose display for Dexcom Share or Nightscout data. Big numbers, clear trend arrows, and no proprietary subscription wall.

~$40 hardware Install Now Setup Guide

Why build it yourself?

The goal is not another app. It is a glanceable display that makes CGM data easier to see across a room without buying an expensive dedicated monitor.

Traditional Solutions SugarClock

Process

From clock to CGM display

Step 1: Start

Pick up an Ulanzi TC001 smart pixel clock and plug it into your computer with a USB-C data cable.

Step 2: Install

Use the browser installer to flash SugarClock firmware and prepare the clock for your Wi-Fi network.

Step 3: Connect

Open the clock's local settings page and add your Dexcom Share or Nightscout configuration.

Feature Cards

Readable glucose values, trend arrows, color-coded ranges, and display modes for different rooms.

Configuration

Adjust glucose source, units, brightness, alerts, timers, weather, and other device settings locally.

User Fit

Designed as a convenience display for caregivers, desks, nightstands, and shared household spaces.

Install

Ready to see your numbers clearly?

Installation runs in your browser on Chrome or Edge desktop. The Mac setup app is available when your browser does not support direct USB installation.

Install with the Mac App

Your browser does not support direct USB installation.

  1. Download and open the SugarClock Setup app on your Mac.
  2. Plug the Ulanzi TC001 into your Mac with a USB-C data cable.
  3. Follow the guided setup flow.

Install SugarClock

SugarClock v0.1.0 - ESP32 - Build 28

  1. Plug the Ulanzi TC001 into your computer with a USB-C data cable.
  2. Click Install and choose the clock when your browser asks for a serial device.
  3. After flashing, follow the display prompt to finish local setup.
You may hear beeping during installation. This is normal and stops once installation completes.

SugarClock is not a medical device. Use your CGM receiver, app, or meter for medical decisions.
